# Dog Care Guidelines

## Feeding
- Adult dogs should be fed 2 times per day, typically morning and evening.
- Puppies under 6 months need 3-4 meals per day.
- Always provide fresh water. Change water at least once daily.
- Avoid feeding dogs chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, or xylitol — these are toxic.
- Common feeding times: 7am and 6pm for adult dogs.

## Exercise and Walks
- Adult dogs need at least 30-60 minutes of exercise per day.
- High-energy breeds (Labs, Huskies, Border Collies) need 1-2 hours daily.
- Puppies: 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, twice a day.
- Senior dogs (7+): shorter, gentler walks. 15-20 minutes twice daily is appropriate.
- Walking after meals helps digestion but wait at least 30 minutes after eating.

## Medications
- Always administer medications at the same time each day for consistency.
- Heartworm prevention: monthly, on the same date each month.
- Flea and tick prevention: monthly or as directed by vet.
- Never give dogs ibuprofen, acetaminophen, or aspirin — these are toxic to dogs.
- If a dog misses a dose, give it as soon as remembered unless it's almost time for the next dose.

## Vet Visits
- Annual wellness exam recommended for all dogs.
- Senior dogs (7+): bi-annual checkups recommended.
- Vaccinations: DHPP booster every 1-3 years depending on vet recommendation.
- Dental cleanings: annually or as recommended.

## Grooming
- Short-haired dogs: brush weekly.
- Long-haired dogs: brush daily to prevent matting.
- Bathe every 4-6 weeks or as needed.
- Trim nails every 3-4 weeks.
- Clean ears weekly to prevent infection.

## Signs of Illness
- Loss of appetite for more than 24 hours.
- Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours.
- Lethargy or unusual behavior.
- Excessive scratching, licking, or chewing.
- Contact a vet immediately if dog ingests a toxic substance.
