# Pet Medication Guidelines

## General Rules
- Always follow vet-prescribed dosages exactly.
- Administer at the same time each day to maintain consistent blood levels.
- Never share medications between pets — doses differ by species and weight.
- Store medications in a cool, dry place away from pets and children.
- Keep a log of medications given to avoid accidental double-dosing.

## Common Dog Medications
- Apoquel (oclacitinib): for allergies. Give with or without food. Once or twice daily.
- Carprofen (Rimadyl): NSAID for pain. Give with food to avoid stomach upset.
- Metronidazole: antibiotic/antiparasitic. Give with food.
- Phenobarbital: for seizures. Never miss a dose — abrupt discontinuation is dangerous.
- Bravecto / NexGard: flea and tick prevention. Monthly or every 3 months depending on product.

## Common Cat Medications
- Prednisolone: steroid for inflammation. Give with food.
- Methimazole: for hyperthyroidism. Wear gloves when handling — absorbed through skin.
- Gabapentin: for pain or anxiety. Can cause sedation — schedule accordingly.
- Onsior (robenacoxib): NSAID for pain. Short-term use only, give with food.

## Toxic Substances — Never Give These
### Dogs
- Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin): causes kidney failure
- Acetaminophen (Tylenol): causes liver failure
- Aspirin: causes GI bleeding
- Xylitol (in sugar-free gum/candy): causes hypoglycemia and liver failure

### Cats
- Ibuprofen: causes kidney failure — extremely dangerous
- Acetaminophen: causes fatal red blood cell damage in cats
- Aspirin: toxic even in small amounts
- Permethrin (in some dog flea treatments): fatal to cats

## Missed Dose Protocol
- If less than half the dosing interval has passed: give the missed dose immediately.
- If more than half has passed: skip and resume normal schedule.
- Never double-dose without vet approval.
- For critical medications (seizure, heart): contact vet immediately if dose is missed.

## Scheduling Tips
- Set phone reminders for daily medications.
- Use a pill organizer or medication log to track doses.
- For twice-daily medications, space doses 10-12 hours apart.
- Morning medications pair well with morning feeding tasks.
