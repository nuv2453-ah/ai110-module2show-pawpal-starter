# Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding
- Adult cats should be fed 2 times per day.
- Kittens under 6 months need 3-4 small meals per day.
- Always provide fresh water. Cats are prone to dehydration.
- Avoid feeding cats onions, garlic, grapes, raisins, chocolate, or xylitol — these are toxic.
- Wet food helps with hydration; dry food alone can lead to urinary issues.

## Exercise and Enrichment
- Indoor cats need at least 15-20 minutes of active play twice daily.
- Use interactive toys: wand toys, laser pointers, puzzle feeders.
- Provide scratching posts to prevent furniture damage and maintain claw health.
- Senior cats (10+): gentler play, shorter sessions.

## Medications
- Administer medications at the same time each day.
- Never give cats ibuprofen, acetaminophen, or aspirin — these are highly toxic to cats.
- Flea prevention: monthly topical or oral treatment.
- Heartworm prevention: monthly, especially for outdoor cats.
- If a cat misses a dose, consult vet guidance before doubling up.

## Vet Visits
- Annual wellness exam for all cats.
- Senior cats (10+): bi-annual checkups.
- Core vaccines: FVRCP every 1-3 years, rabies as required by law.
- Dental checkups: annually.

## Grooming
- Short-haired cats: brush weekly.
- Long-haired cats: brush daily to prevent hairballs and matting.
- Cats generally self-groom but may need help with mats.
- Trim nails every 2-3 weeks.

## Signs of Illness
- Loss of appetite for more than 24 hours.
- Changes in litter box habits (straining, blood, or not using it).
- Excessive hiding or lethargy.
- Rapid breathing or open-mouth breathing.
- Contact a vet immediately for any suspected poisoning.
